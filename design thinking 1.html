Design Thinking - Class 1: Inception



In our first design thinking class, "Inception," we explored how design can be used to tackle real-world challenges with focus and intention. The professor highlighted ideas like maintaining consistent effort ("Ostinato Rigor") and considering the broader impact of our work ("Memento Mori"). We were encouraged to keep track of our progress and take responsibility for our ideas. The session wrapped up with team-building activities to foster collaboration and prepare us for group work.





Our first class, called "Inception," began with a key question: What is design? When asked, I said, “Design is everything.” This led to a discussion about the many different types of design, from visual elements to the practical and structural parts of our everyday life. We explored how design isn't just about making things look good, but also about finding solutions to real-world problems. This gave us a new perspective on the course, showing that design is more than just theory it's a tool for solving problems.
